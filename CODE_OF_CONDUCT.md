# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

This project is committed to providing a welcoming, safe, and constructive
environment for everyone who participates. Participation includes opening
issues, proposing changes, reviewing pull requests, discussing design choices,
and interacting in any project-managed space.

## Expected behavior

Participants are expected to:

- communicate respectfully and focus feedback on ideas and code;
- assume good intent while asking for clarification when needed;
- accept constructive feedback and correct mistakes;
- respect different levels of experience and different perspectives;
- protect private, personal, and security-sensitive information; and
- follow project policies, GitHub policies, and applicable law.

## Unacceptable behavior

The following behavior is not permitted:

- harassment, discrimination, threats, intimidation, or personal attacks;
- sexualized language, imagery, or unwanted attention;
- deliberate disruption, trolling, or repeated off-topic arguments;
- publishing another person's private information without permission;
- sharing credentials, private resume data, or undisclosed vulnerabilities;
- impersonation or intentionally misleading claims; and
- retaliation against someone who reports a concern in good faith.

## Scope

This policy applies in the repository and in any public or private space where
someone is representing the project.

## Reporting and enforcement

For ordinary project disagreements, open an issue and keep the report factual.
For sensitive conduct concerns, contact the maintainer through the contact
options on the [exitLQ GitHub profile](https://github.com/exitLQ) without posting
private details publicly.

The maintainer may edit or remove content, close or lock discussions, reject
contributions, issue a warning, or temporarily or permanently restrict
participation. Enforcement decisions will consider the context, impact,
severity, and pattern of behavior.

Reports will be reviewed as privately and promptly as the available information
allows. People who report concerns in good faith must not be subjected to
retaliation.
